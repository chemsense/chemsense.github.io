Welcome

The ChemSense lab aims to address detection challenges related to sensitivity, selectivity, portability, and availability in environmental preservation and human health. We strive to accomplish this by developing cutting-edge chemical sensing devices and chemical analysis methods.

  • 10/21/2024: Our paper "Ionophore-Based SERS Sensing for Electrolyte Cations" was accepted in Analytical Chemistry for publication.

    • In this paper, we demonstrated the feasibility of using SERS for ion sensing with Chromoionophore I (CHI) as the indicator. We also elucidated the mechanism behind the variation in SERS intensity of CHI during the sensing process.
